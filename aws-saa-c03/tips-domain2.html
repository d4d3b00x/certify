Designing for resilience

Resilience in SAA focuses on redundancy + automation. Distribute across AZs, remove single points of failure, and let managed services heal automatically.

Patterns that score

  • Stateless frontends in multiple AZs behind ALB; externalize session state.
  • Managed replication: RDS Multi‑AZ, Aurora replicas, DynamoDB global tables.
  • Decoupling: buffer with SQS; stream & replay with Kinesis.
  • Global designs: Route 53 latency‑based routing + health checks; S3 CRR + CloudFront failover.
Tip: When RTO/RPO is strict, think multi‑Region active‑active and globally replicated data.

Pitfalls

  • One ALB/ASG in a single AZ — no failover capacity.
  • Database single‑AZ with manual backups counted as HA.
  • Tight coupling between producers and consumers (no buffering).

Study checklist

  • Compare DR strategies and when to choose each (cost vs RTO/RPO).
  • Target group health checks & deregistration delay (connection draining).
  • Route 53 policies: latency, weighted, failover, multi‑value.
  • Kinesis shards, retention, checkpointing, enhanced fan‑out.

Exam Focus

  • Multi‑AZ patterns for stateless + stateful tiers
  • Decouple with SQS/SNS and streaming with Kinesis
  • DR strategies (backup/restore, pilot light, warm standby, multi‑site)
  • Multi‑Region designs with Route 53 health checks & latency routing

Key AWS services

  • ALB/NLB, Auto Scaling groups, Target groups & health checks
  • RDS Multi‑AZ / Aurora (replicas, Global Database)
  • DynamoDB Global Tables, S3 CRR + CloudFront origin failover
  • Route 53 routing policies, Global Accelerator
  • Kinesis Data Streams/Firehose, SQS, SNS, EventBridge

Hands‑on ideas

  • ASG across two AZs behind ALB with health checks and warm pools
  • RDS Multi‑AZ failover test; add RDS Proxy for connection pooling
  • S3 CRR with CloudFront origin group for failover
  • Kinesis stream with enhanced fan‑out and consumer app catch‑up
